About the Initiative

Light Footsteps is a non-profit organization that enables hospitalized youth with chronic diseases to continue pursuing their interests in the arts, sports, and dance. Light Footsteps focuses on empowering hospitalized youth so that they will have the self confidence to succeed later in life through the physical, mental, and emotional benefits of cultivating their talents.

The initaitive started in late 2013 with only a dance program, and is currently in five hospitals in Thailand and, with the University of Health Sciences, is expanding into Cambodia.

Believe

We believe in passion. We believe in getting lost in plotlines, writing plotlines, the inherent freedom of every single one of the 7 billion that occupy this earth to feel empowered to pursue whatever their dreams desire, from creative writing to dance to sports and changing the world.

We believe in recovery and willpower. We believe that hospitalized youth, especially those admitted with chronic or terminal illnesses, should not only receive the bare necessities of physical health, but be able to pursue any passions and dreams they may harbor before disease overcame them. Don't let their disease define them.

We believe in identity, being able to hold on to the things and hobbies that fill our hearts with joy, regardless of our condition. We believe that there is no "us" and "them," the hospitalized youth.

They may be blocked from the general public's view, blocked watching the streets and people walking below from their windows above. They may have physical and mental deformities. But they are not monster-like abnormalities. There is no "us" and "them."

There is only we.

Activities

We have curriculae in dance, music, performing arts, and sports, tailored towards the medical conditions of our youth. LFI matches volunteers to youth in Thai hospitals based on interests for weekly and bi-weekly one-on-ones, where volunteers help youth advance their skills and passions in the above tracks.

LFI advocates and stands alongside other anti-stigmatization and disability rights organizations in Thailand, and also spreads awareness of the accomplishments of our youth in order to decrease this stigma. We give our youth the opportunity to showcase their talents in plays and musical preformances, which are open to the public.

We currently opeerate in five hospitals in Thailand, and in collaboration with the University of Health Sciences in Cambodia.

Light Footsteps is the first on-going organization of such a scale to be founded by a Thai teenager. Other than helping break down the stigma of hospitalized youth, we are breaking down the stigmas and barriers for youth to create change and share their ideas. We have been recognized and seen on Tedx, We Are Family Foundation, YouthCorp, and the Queen of Thailand, one of the highest national honors possible.
